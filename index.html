If it's your first time

Here's what to know.

01

What to wear

Comfortable clothes and soft-soled shoes you can move in. No heels, no flip flops.

02

When to arrive

10–15 minutes early. Settle in, say hi, meet a few people before class starts.

03

Need a partner?

No. Come solo. We rotate partners throughout class — everyone dances with everyone.

04

Parking

Free, on site. The door is on Pitkin Street — come straight in, we'll be there.

Pedro Castro & Ana Reis

Lead Instructors · 2024 World Champions

Pedro and Ana grew up dancing in Rio and trained at Alex de Carvalho's school — where Brazil's best Zouk teachers come from. In 2024 they won the Brazilian Zouk World Championship, and now they teach here every week.

They break the dance down with the patience of people who've lived inside it their whole lives, focusing on connection — the thing that makes Zouk feel unlike any other partner dance. They make it click fast.
