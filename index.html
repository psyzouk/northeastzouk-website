Our Story

A small studio with a big lineage.

Kaylyn Morton and JR Lam have been dancing Brazilian Zouk together for nearly ten years. As the dance grew deeper in their lives, a question kept surfacing: why isn't this here? Why do you have to fly to a major city — or to Brazil — just to learn from someone who really knows it?

"The more people dancing Zouk, the better. That means making it easy to start."

That belief led them to Pedro Castro and Ana Reis — 2024 Brazilian Zouk World Champions, trained at Centro de Dança Alex de Carvalho in Rio — and to bringing them to Western New York as resident instructors. It also led to an uncommon decision: Wednesday Level 1 is free. Every week. For anyone who walks in. The school is community-funded so the teaching stays world-class and the door stays open.

If it's your first time

Here's what to know.

01

What to wear

Comfortable clothes and soft-soled shoes you can move in. No heels, no flip flops.

02

When to arrive

10–15 minutes early. Settle in, say hi, meet a few people before class starts.

03

Need a partner?

No. Come solo. We rotate partners throughout class — everyone dances with everyone.

04

Parking

Free, on site. The door is on Pitkin Street — come straight in, we'll be there.

Pedro Castro

Lead Instructor

Pedro grew up in Rio and trained at Alex de Carvalho's school — where Brazil's best Zouk teachers come from. He won the 2024 World Championship. He teaches technique with the patience of someone who's been living inside this dance his whole life.

Ana Reis

Lead Instructor

Ana has been dancing since she was five. She trained in Rio, won the 2024 World Championship, and now spends her weeks teaching connection — the thing that makes Zouk feel unlike every other partner dance you've tried. She makes it click fast.
