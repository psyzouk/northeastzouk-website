Our Story

A small studio with a big lineage.

For Kaylyn Morton and JR Lam, Brazilian Zouk has been a cornerstone of the last ten years — a hobby that quietly became a way of life. They've danced in Toronto, in LA, and all around the Zouk circuit, following the music and the connection wherever it led.

"The more people dancing Zouk, the better. That means making it easy to start."

Eventually they wanted to plant roots — to build a home for this dance instead of always traveling to find it. That's Northeast Zouk. And it's why Pedro Castro and Ana Reis, 2024 Brazilian Zouk World Champions, are now in year two of a residency across Western New York with us — teaching every week, and keeping Wednesday nights free and open to anyone who walks in.

The more people dancing Zouk, the better.
That means the door stays open.

Wednesday Level 1 is free every week — not as a promotion, but as a principle. The school is community-funded so the teaching stays world-class and the door stays open.

If it's your first time

Here's what to know.

01

What to wear

Comfortable clothes and soft-soled shoes you can move in. No heels, no flip flops.

02

When to arrive

10–15 minutes early. Settle in, say hi, meet a few people before class starts.

03

Need a partner?

No. Come solo. We rotate partners throughout class — everyone dances with everyone.

04

Parking

Free, on site. The door is on Pitkin Street — come straight in, we'll be there.

Community standards

Northeast Zouk is an explicitly welcoming space. We ask everyone — regardless of experience, background, or who they came with — to treat every partner with care and to accept a "no thank you" without question or comment. Consent to one dance is not consent to another.

If anything in class ever makes you uncomfortable, tell Kaylyn, JR, Pedro, or Ana directly — or email us at dancing@northeastzouk.com. We take it seriously.
